Our primary purpose is to provide a safe and stable living environment for children who would otherwise be without a family. This includes providing them with food, clothing, and medical care, as well as a place to sleep and learn. Orphanages also aim to provide children with a sense of community and belonging, through activities and interactions with other children and staff members.

In addition to basic needs, Pasaydan also aims to offer educational and recreational activities to help children develop socially, emotionally, and intellectually. We also provide counseling and therapy services to help children cope with the trauma of losing their parents and adjusting to life in an orphanage.

Our Services

Pasaydan Seva Prakalp is a non-governmental organization that works towards improving the lives of orphans and underprivileged children.

Basic Necessities

The NGO is committed to providing orphans and underprivileged the basic necessities of life, including food, clothing and education.

Healthcare

The NGO, along with fulfilling the basic necessities of children, also provides healthcare facilities to them. This helps reduce health risks of our children.

Our Team

Pasaydan Seva Prakalp Started their journey with Gowardhan Darade in 2009.

Mr. Gowardhan Darade

Owner of Pasaydan Seva Prakalp
I, Gowardhan Darade, am the founder of Pasaydan Seva Prakalp, an NGO dedicated to helping children who are orphaned, affected by natural disasters, have lost their families due to COVID-19, whose parents have committed suicide, and whose fathers are in jail. My journey to starting Pasaydan Seva Prakalp began in my childhood. I was raised in a small village called Khadki (Devla) in the hills of Balaghat in Beed district. Growing up, my family was poor, and I faced many of the same struggles that the children I now help face.
Despite the odds against me, I was determined to succeed. I worked hard and was able to pass my 10th grade, at the age of 16, I started working with a sickle. I continued cutting sugarcane for 2 long years, which was a family tradition. I shifted from being a sugarcane cutter to a vehicle cleaner, and then became an in-charge of a company. I struggled for years and learned many things, but I always felt conscious, always thinking that the environment I grew up in, I didn't want anyone else to face that kind of situation anymore. This was when I knew that I had to take the task of shaping their lives into my own hands. Around that time, I left my well-paying job and started Pasaydan Seva Prakalp in 2009. My goal was to help children from farmers who have committed suicide in Marathwada, children of sugarcane workers, the children of those who earn their living by collecting garbage and scraps in the city, and the destitute children who are underprivileged. I even sold 2 acres of land to make that place for children in Beed District.
As the founder and head of Pasaydan Seva Prakalp, I work tirelessly to ensure that the children under my care have access to the best possible facilities and opportunities. I organize various tasks to keep them engaged and motivated, and I go to great lengths to gather resources, such as food, to ensure that they have everything they need to thrive. I am proud of the progress and impact we have made so far and I will continue to do my best to shape their lives in the best way possible. The success of the organization is a result of kind helping hands and I am grateful for the support received.
